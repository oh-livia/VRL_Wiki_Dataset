1 The Great Crested Flycatcher is a large insect-eating bird of the tyrant flycatcher family.
0 It is the most widespread member of the genus, Myiarchus, in North America and is found over most of the eastern and mid-western portions of the continent.
0 It dwells mostly in the treetops and rarely found on the ground.
1 Adult Great Crested Flycatchers usually measure between 17–21 cm in length with a wingspan of around 34 cm .
1 This bird usually weighs between 27–40 g .
0 The Great Crested Flycatcher does not display sexual dimorphism.
1 Adults are brownish on the upperparts with yellow underparts; they have a long rusty brown tail and a bushy crest.
1 Their throat and breast are grey.
0 Their breeding habitat is deciduous or mixed forests across eastern North America.
0 They nest in a cavity in a tree.
0 A snake skin is usually included in the lining of the nest; sometimes a plastic wrapper is substituted.
0 These birds migrate to Mexico and South America, also Florida and Cuba.
0 They wait on a high perch and fly out to catch insects in flight, sometimes hovering to pick food off vegetation.
0 They also eat fruits and berries.
0 This bird's call is a whistled weep.
