0 The Seaside Sparrow, Ammodramus maritimus, is a small American sparrow.
0 The 11 Ammodramus species inhabit marshes and grasslands.
1 Adults have brownish upperparts with grey on the crown and nape, and a grayish buff colored breast with dark streaks; they have a dark face with grey cheeks, a white throat, and a short pointed tail.
1 Birds show a small yellow streak just above the eye.
0 Their breeding habitat is salt marshes on the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States from southern New Hampshire to southern Texas.
0 The nest is an open cup usually built in the salt marsh on tidal reeds and spartina grasses.
0 Females lay 2-5 eggs.
0 Northern birds most often migrate further south along the eastern coast of the United States.
0 They forage on the ground or in marsh vegetation, sometimes probing in mud.
0 They mainly eat insects, marine invertebrates and seeds.
0 Their feeding areas are often some distance away from the areas they choose to nest.
0 One of the numerous subspecies of this bird, the Dusky Seaside Sparrow , has recently become extinct, and the Cape Sable subspecies, A. m. mirabilis, is endangered.
