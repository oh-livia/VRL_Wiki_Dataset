1 The Slaty-backed Gull , is a large white-headed gull that breeds on the western coast of Alaska but travels widely during nonbreeding seasons.
0 Claims have been made as to its presence throughout North America as well as the eastern coast of Asia.
1 It is similar in appearance to the Western Gull and the Glaucous-winged Gull.
0 Another alternate name is Pacific Gull, though this also applies to a southern hemisphere species, Larus pacificus.
1 The Slaty-backed Gull is a very large gull, measuring 55-68.5cm in length, 132-160cm in wingspan and weighing 1.05-1.7kg.
1 It has a white head, belly, and tail with a dark slaty-gray back and wings with a broad white trailing edge.
1 The wings and back are slightly darker than those of the Western Gull.
1 The underside of the wings features a "string of pearls" pattern along their edges; these can be seen from below when the bird is in flight.
1 Its eyes are yellow.
1 The legs are pink and short when compared with those of similar-looking gulls, and the body appears more stout.
1 The bill is yellow with orange-red subterminal spot.
1 Immature gulls' plumage is brown, similar to the that of the Great Black-backed Gull, but paler, and is practically indistinguishable from the immature Herring Gull in the field.
0 Like other gulls, the Slaty-backed Gull is both a forager and a scavenger.
