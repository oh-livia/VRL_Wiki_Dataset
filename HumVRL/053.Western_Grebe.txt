0 The Western Grebe, , is a species in the grebe family of water birds.
0 Folk names include "dabchick", "swan grebe" and "swan-necked grebe".
1 This is the largest North American grebe.
1 It is 55-75cm long, weighs 795-1818g and measures 79-102cm across the wings.
1 It is black-and-white, with a long, slender, swan-like neck and red eyes.
0 It is easily confused with Clark's Grebe, which shares the same features, behavior and habitat, and hybrids are known.
1 The Western Grebe has black around the eyes and a straight greenish-yellow bill whereas the Clark's Grebe has white around the eyes and an up-turned bright yellow bill.
1 The downy young of Western are grey; Clark's downy young are white.
0 Western Grebes nest in colonies of hundreds on large inland lakes, sometimes using coastal marshes, in western North America.
0 It has a spectacular courtship display; two birds will rear up and patter across the water's surface.
0 Northern birds migrate west to coastal ocean in winter; birds in the southwest and Mexico may be permanent residents.
0 This bird dines by diving for carp, herring, mollusks, crabs, and salamanders.
0 Western Grebe fossils from the Late Pleistocene of SW North America were described as a distinct species , but later ranked as a paleosubspecies Aechmophorus occidentalis lucasi .
