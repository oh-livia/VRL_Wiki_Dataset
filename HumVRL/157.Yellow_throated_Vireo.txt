1 The Yellow-throated Vireo, Vireo flavifrons, is a small American songbird.
1 Adults are mainly olive on the head and upperparts with a yellow throat and white belly; they have dark eyes with yellow "spectacles".
1 The tail and wings are dark with white wing bars.
1 They have thick blue-grey legs and a stout bill.
0 Their breeding habitat is open deciduous woods in southern Canada and the eastern United States.
0 These birds migrate to the deep southern United States, Mexico and Central America.
0 They are very rare vagrants to western Europe.
0 There is one record from Britain in Kenidjack Valley Cornwall September 20-27 1990.
0 There is also a sight report from Germany.
0 They forage for insects high in trees.
0 They also eat berries, especially before migration and in winter when they are occasionally seen feeding on Gumbo-limbo fruit.
0 They make a thick cup nest attached to a fork in a tree branch.
