1 The American Tree Sparrow , formerly known as the Winter Sparrow, is a medium-sized sparrow.
1 Adults have a rusty cap and grey underparts with a small dark spot on the breast.
1 They have a rusty back with lighter stripes, brown wings with white bars and a slim tail.
1 Their face is grey with a rusty line through the eye.
1 Their flanks are splashed with light brown.
0 They are similar in appearance to the Chipping Sparrow.
0 Their breeding habitat is tundra or the northern limits of the boreal forest in Alaska and northern Canada.
0 They nest on the ground.
0 These birds migrate to the United States or southern Canada to spend the winter.
0 Usually, Chipping Sparrows are moving south around the same time as these birds arrive.
0 These birds forage on the ground or in low bushes, often in flocks when not nesting.
0 They mainly eat seeds and insects, some berries.
0 They are commonly seen near feeders with Dark-eyed Juncos.
0 This bird's song is a sweet high warble descending in pitch and becoming buzzy near the finish.
