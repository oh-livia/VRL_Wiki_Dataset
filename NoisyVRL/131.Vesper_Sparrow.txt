0 The Vesper Sparrow is a medium-sized American sparrow.
0 It is the only member of the genus Pooecetes.
0 Adults have light brown upperparts and light underparts, both with darker streaking.
0 They have a white eye ring and a long dark brown tail which shows white outer feathers in flight.
0 Their breeding habitat is open grassy areas across most of North America.
0 The nest is an open cup on the ground under a clump of grass.
0 These birds migrate to the southern and central United States and Mexico.
0 These birds forage on the ground, mainly eating insects and seeds.
0 Outside the nesting season they often feed in small flocks.
0 The male sings from a higher perch, such as a shrub or fencepost, which indicates his ownership of the nesting territory.
0 The musical song begins with two pairs of repeated whistled notes and ends in a series of trills, somewhat similar to that of the Song Sparrow.
0 This bird's numbers are declining in the eastern parts of its range due to habitat loss.
