0 The Rock Wren is a small songbird of the wren family.
0 It is the only species in the genus Salpinctes.
0 The 12 cm long adults have grey-brown upperparts with small black and white spots and pale grey underparts with a light brown rump.
0 They have a light grey line over the eye, a long slightly decurved thin bill, a long barred tail and dark legs.
0 Their breeding habitat is dry rocky locations, including canyons, from southwestern Canada south to Costa Rica.
0 This bird builds a cup nest in a crevice or cavity, usually among rocks.
0 These birds are permanent residents in the south of their range, but northern populations migrate to warmer areas from the central United States and southwest Canada southwards.
0 They are occasional vagrants in the eastern United States.
0 These birds actively hunt on the ground, around and under objects, probing with their bill as their extraction tool.
0 They mainly eat insects and spiders.
0 This bird's song is a trill, becoming more varied during the nesting season.
