0 The Mourning Warbler, Oporornis philadelphia, is a small songbird of the New World warbler family.
0 These 13 cm long birds have yellow underparts, olive-green upperparts and pink legs.
0 Adult males have a grey hood and a black patch on the throat and breast.
0 Females and immatures are grey-brown on the head with an incomplete eye-ring.
0 Their breeding habitat is thickets and semi-open areas with dense shrubs across Canada east of the Rockies and the northeastern United States.
0 The nest is an open cup placed on the ground in a well-concealed location under thick shrubs or other vegetation.
0 These birds migrate to Central America and northern South America.
0 They forage low in vegetation, sometimes catching insects in flight.
0 These birds mainly eat insects, also some plant material in winter.
0 The song of this bird is a bright repetitive warble.
0 The call is a sharp chip.
0 The "mourning" in this bird's name refers to the male's hood, thought to resemble a mourning veil.
