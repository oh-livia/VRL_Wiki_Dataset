0 The Black-throated Blue Warbler, Setophaga caerulescens, is a small songbird of the New World warbler family.
1 Adult males have white underparts with black throat, face and flanks; the upperparts are deep blue; immature males are similar with upperparts more greenish.
1 Females have olive-brown upperparts and light yellow underparts with darker wings and tail, a grey crown and a brown patch on the cheek.
1 All birds have a small white wing patch which is not always visible, and a thin pointed bill.
1 Like many warblers, this bird has colorful plumage during the spring and summer, but its fall plumage is drab and less distinctive.
1 In the fall, it can still be identified from other similar warblers by its small white wing patch.
0 Their breeding habitat is located in the interior of deciduous and mixed forests in eastern North America.
0 Their nests are open cups placed in thick shrubs.
0 Their close to ground nesting sites make the Black Throated Blue Warbler a favoured species for the study of warbler behavior in the wild.
0 These birds migrate to islands in the Caribbean and Central America.
0 They defend their territory against other birds of the same species for both nesting and winter habitats.
0 The Black-throated Blue Warbler is a very rare vagrant to western Europe.
0 These birds forage actively in low vegetation, sometimes hovering or catching insects in flight.
0 Blue-throated Black Warblers often forage in one area for a while before moving on to the next.
0 These birds mainly eat insects, including caterpillars and crane flies, and spiders.
0 Seeds, berries and fruit may be added to their diets in winter.
0 The song of this bird is a buzzed zee-zee-zeeee with an upward inflection.
0 The call is a flat ctuk.
0 As this bird requires large, unbroken forest areas for nesting, its numbers are declining.
