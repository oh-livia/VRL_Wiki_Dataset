0 The Western Wood-Pewee, Contopus sordidulus, is a small tyrant flycatcher.
0 Adults are gray-olive on the upperparts with light underparts, washed with olive on the breast.
0 They have two wing bars and a dark bill with yellow at the base of the lower mandible.
0 This bird is very similar in appearance to the Eastern Wood-Pewee; the two birds were formerly considered to be one species.
0 The call of C. sordidulus is a loud buzzy peeer; the song consists of three rapid descending tsees ending with a descending peeer.
0 Their breeding habitat is open wooded areas in western North America.
0 These birds migrate to South America at the end of summer.
0 The female lays two or three eggs in an open cup nest on a horizontal tree branch or within a tree cavity; California black oak forests are examples of suitable nesting habitat for this species of bird.
0 Both parents feed the young.
0 They wait on a perch at a middle height in a tree and fly out to catch insects in flight , sometimes hovering to pick insects from vegetation .
