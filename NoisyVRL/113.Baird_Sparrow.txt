0 The Baird's Sparrow, Ammodramus bairdii, is a small North American sparrow.
1 These birds have a large bill, a large flat head, and a short forked tail.
1 They have brown upper parts and white underparts, with streaking on the back, breast, and flanks.
1 The face, nape, and crown stripe are yellowish.
0 Their breeding habitat is tall grass prairie regions in southern central Canada and the northern mid-western United States.
0 The nest is an open cup in a well-hidden grassy location on the ground.
0 These birds migrate to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
0 They forage on the ground, mainly eating insects in summer and seeds in winter.
0 These birds usually nest in small loose colonies.
0 Males sing from perches within their nesting territory.
0 The song consists of a short series of high notes followed by a trill.
0 This bird is more often seen than heard.
0 This bird's numbers have decreased with the loss of suitable habitat.
0 This bird was named after Spencer Fullerton Baird, an American naturalist.
