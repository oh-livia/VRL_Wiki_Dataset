0 The Orange-crowned Warbler is a small songbird of the New World warbler family.
1 These birds are distinguished by their lack of wing bars, streaking on the underparts, strong face marking or bright colouring, resembling a fall Tennessee Warbler.
1 The orange patch on the crown is usually not visible.
1 They have olive-grey upperparts, yellowish underparts with faint streaking and a thin pointed bill.
1 They have a faint line over their eyes and a faint broken eye ring.
1 Females and immatures are duller in colour than males.
1 Western birds are yellower than eastern birds.
0 Their breeding habitat is open shrubby areas across Canada, Alaska and the western United States.
0 The nest is a small open cup well-concealed on the ground under vegetation or low in shrubs.
0 The female builds the nest; both parents feed the young.
0 These birds migrate to the southern United States and south to Central America.
0 They forage actively in low shrubs, flying from perch to perch, sometimes hovering.
0 These birds eat insects, berries and nectar.
0 They also enjoy peanut butter.
0 The song of this bird is a trill, descending in pitch and volume.
0 The call is a high chip.
