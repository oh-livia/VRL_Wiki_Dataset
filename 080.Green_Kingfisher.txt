0 The Green Kingfisher, Chloroceryle americana, is a resident breeding bird which occurs from southern Texas in the USA south through Central and South Americal to central Argentina.
0 This small kingfisher breeds by streams in forests or mangroves.
0 The nest is in a horizontal tunnel up to a metre long made in a river bank.
0 The female lays three, sometimes four, eggs.
0 The Green Kingfisher is 19cm long and weighs 27g.
0 It has the typical kingfisher shape, with a short tail and long bill.
0 It is oily green above, with white markings on the wings and tail, and a white collar around the neck.
0 Males have white underparts apart from a broad chestnut breast band and some green spotting on the flanks.
0 Females have buff-white underparts with two green chest bands, the lower of which links to the green spotting along the sides of the belly.
0 Green Kingfishers are often seen perched on a low shaded branch close to water before plunging in head first after their fish prey.
0 They also eat aquatic insects.
0 These birds often give a pebbly rattling call.
0 Variation in this species is clinal.
0 There are five subspecies: Birds occurring on Trinidad and Tobago, usually included in americana, have a larger and heavier bill than the mainland forms, and are sometimes separated as subspecies croteta.
